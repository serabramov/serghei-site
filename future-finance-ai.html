The Future of Finance and Controlling in the Age of AI

Artificial intelligence is starting to change how finance teams work. Not because AI replaces finance professionals, but because it can process large amounts of information much faster than humans.

In the last year several AI companies have pushed this development forward — for example Anthropic with Claude, OpenAI with ChatGPT and Palantir with data-driven operational platforms.

These tools are not accounting systems. But they are increasingly capable of reading reports, summarizing information and helping analysts explore data faster.

For finance and controlling teams this could shift the focus of work. Less time may be spent preparing reports, and more time explaining what the numbers actually mean for the business.

AI can highlight patterns and summarize operational updates. But financial judgement — understanding strategy, risks and context — still belongs to people.

In practice this means finance professionals may gradually move closer to the role of decision support architects rather than report producers.

The real question is therefore not whether AI will replace finance teams, but how finance teams will integrate AI into their analytical workflows.
